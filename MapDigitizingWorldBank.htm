ERDAS Image Processing & GIS systems are in use organizations and institutions - academic, commercial, and governmental - all over the world. I don't intend to over-emphasize my role with the particular system installed at the World Bank, although I did work on-site there to develop a custom driver for their map digitizing tablet. Rather, I'm using this one installation as representative of all the different systems around the world which used my map digitizing software, map projections software, and Arc/Info Live Link driver.

For all of the gee-whiz aspects of these applications and for all the impressive uses to which they have been put, I consider the most technically significant aspect of this work to be that the software lived and breathed and evolved through numerous versions and thousands of users over the period of time of a decade. Consider for a moment the technical changes in computer systems, in operating systems, and in programming languages that occur over a decade in this industry. Only professional software, which is designed well and coded to exacting standards is able to perform at that level for that long.
