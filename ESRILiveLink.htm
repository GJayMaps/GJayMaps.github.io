ESRI is the leader in mapping and vector-based GIS. ERDAS is the leader in image processing and raster-based GIS. The ESRI-ERDAS Live Link was the first commercially available product to geographically register and display a vector map over an image backdrop such as a satellite image or aerial photo.

All complex projects are the result of collaborative effort by many talented individuals. In the case of the Live Link product, without exaggeration or boasting, I'll take sole credit for conceiving, proposing, designing, specing, and developing this capability. A dozen years later, when such maps are omnipresent on the web, on portable PCs, and in rental cars, it is not often that such a capability can be traced to a single developer.

There was a great deal more to this product than just registering a map to an image. Both software packages, Arc/Info and ERDAS ran in full capacity in their native environments. They shared geographic information in runtime through a shared-memory area. Thus they notified each other of commands and options the user had selected, so the other package could automatically respond with an appropriate action. Remember that this was all before the 90's event-driven and client-server programming paradigms. In fact, it was even made to work on 640K DOS, as well as on various flavors of Unix, VMS, and for God's sake even PRIMOS.
