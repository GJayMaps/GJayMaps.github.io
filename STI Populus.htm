POPULUS

 

Spatial Technologies, Inc.

Atlanta, GA

 

1989-1993

 

Griff Jay

gcon@peak.org

POPULUS was used by school districts to map student location and attendance boundaries. POPULUS was used by commercial banks to map the distribution of loans by census tracts. With POPULUS, school districts were able to meet court mandated desegregation requirements. With POPULUS, banks met Federal Community Reinvestment Act requirements and passed Federal CRA audits.

POPULUS used the U.S. Census DIME maps and TIGER files to geocode administrative databases. With X,Y coordinates attached to each record, POPULUS then plotted dot distribution maps using user-drawn district boundaries to produce summary statistics. It is significant that POPULUS was used by end-user analysts, who where not computer or GIS specialists, and that was before the days of current desktop mapping applications.

The early POPULUS versions were DOS/Fortran and the later versions were translated into C and Win 3.1.
