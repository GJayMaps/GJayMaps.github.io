FEED - Field Exploitation of Elevation Data

 

GTRI

Atlanta, GA

U.S. Army Topographic Laboratory

Ft. Belvoir, VA

 

1981-1984

 

Griff Jay

gcon@peak.org

The FEED project was a joint research effort between GTRI and the U.S. Army's Topographic Laboratory to demonstrate how digital elevation data could be applied in operational units in the field. A mini-computer system was developed for a mobile van, and digital terrain databases were prepared for several locales where exercises are regularly conducted. The FEED van was on demonstration or participated in exercises at Ft. Belvoir, VA, Ft. Bragg, NC, Ft. Huachuca, AZ, and HdqtrsCINCPACCOM, Honolulu, HI.

Landsat satellite imagery and digital othophotos were draped over the digital terrain databases producing a 3-dimensional view from the geographic position and elevation of the viewer. The user could interactively traverse the digital landscape. The system was used to plot position of units, make line-of-sight calculations, and perform view-shed analysis.
