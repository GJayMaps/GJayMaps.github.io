Griff Jay

gcon@peak.org

Research Scientist positions at GTRI were faculty-level, non-tenure track applied research and development positions. Organized into independent labs, we sought sponsored research, we marketed our capabilities, we designed solutions for the clients needs, we spec'ed the statements of work, we performed the development and then documented the results, delivered on-site, and trained the end-users.

I'm proud of the fact that as a self-trained programmer with a City Planner degree and an undergraduate liberal arts degree, I hired into the Radar Laboratory. I'll always give Dr. Reedy, the Radar Lab Director, great credit for knowing that he wanted a more diverse staff than just a bunch of EEs walking around. Well, many of those EEs were world-class talents whom I'm still in awe of their capabilities, and I was flattered to learn that they needed me to turn their often difficult to interpret B-scope radar displays into intuitive geographic maps displays. Following that, in the Electromagnetics Laboratory, I worked on applications in remote sensing, image processing, digital topographic data, GIS, and raster image / vector map integration.

Please see: Pt. Mugu NAS Radar Modeling, Naval Coastal Systems Center WIDS, State of Alaska Geochemical Modeling System, IBM Graphics Driver, ERDAS Digitizing & Mapping, Army Topographic Laboratory FEED System
