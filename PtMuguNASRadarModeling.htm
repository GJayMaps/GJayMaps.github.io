When Ronald Reagan was President, he flew on Air Force One into and out of the Pt. Mugu Naval Air Station. Because of the high surrounding mountains, there was a blind spot in the FAA's In-Route Air Traffic Control system. GTRI was contracted to produce a radar model for the base and to recommend an optimum site for a new radar placement that would eliminate the blind spot.

I was the lead programmer on the project. We obtained DEMs (Digital Elevation Model) from the U. S. Geological Survey and DTED data (Digital Terrain Elevation Data) from the Defense Mapping Agency. We calculated line of sight for a 360 degree radar sweep to the peak elevations, i.e. the ridge line surrounding the base.

We also produced a geographic map of the base and a three-dimensional CAD model of all the NAS buildings, fences, towers, etc., i.e any structure that would reflect a radar beam. We contracted with the GTRI Radar Lab for a radar model to measure radar reflection and false targets. Using the elevation data, the geographic map, the CAD model, and the radar reflection model, we identified two on-base sites which eliminated the blind spots and minimized radar reflection.
