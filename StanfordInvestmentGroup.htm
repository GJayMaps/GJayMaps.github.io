The Stanford Investment Group is a boutique investment planning firm, advising and managing the wealth of Silicon Valley executives and entrepreneurs for 25 years. No, it is not affiliated with Stanford University, as one might assume from the name. However, it's founders and present key executives individually have close association with the university.

I support SIG's mission critical applications for portfolio allocation, financial planning, retirement planning, and corporate billing. "Support" is the correct word now; "resurrect" is a better term for what was needed in 2007. The previous developer was a financial planning expert who fancied himself a programmer. What he was is a hack who left extremely complex applications in shambles and, of course in character, without any documentation.

The apps are in Excel VBA. I implemented a U/I standard across all the programs, instituted error checking (of which there was none), wrote programmers's and user's documentation, and attended to the hundreds of details that make software intuitive, stable, and professional.

My goal for SIG is to build an in-house semantic web database, which can capture the complexities of business activities and swirl of staff interactions and then be used to simply and intuitively assist all employees in managing their daily tasks and everyday new challenges.
