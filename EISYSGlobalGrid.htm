In the S.O.M Global Grid project, I implemented the Space Oblique Mercator map projection so that the S.O.M. transformation could be performed on digital maps, thus allowing the maps to be overlaid directly onto un-rectified Landsat satellite imagery. Landsat images are quite large, and using the computer processing power available during that era, the conventional rectification process would be an exceedingly slow (all-day and overnight) batch process. With the Global Grid application, EISYS was able to correctly georeference vector maps in a matter of minutes to any Landsat scene on any part of the earth.

EISYS Corp. (purchased by 3DI Inc in 1999) is a consulting firm expert in remote sensing, image processing, and GIS.

Other EISYS projects: Kuala Kencana (Indonesia) GIS, ARCOView Field Data Collection
