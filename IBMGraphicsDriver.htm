Graphics Driver

Device Independent 2-D & 3-D Graphics Package

 

IBM

Rochester, MN

 

1982

 

Griff Jay

gcon@peak.org

This project allowed IBM to market graphics capabilities for one of its early business-grade dot-matrix printers. At GTRI we had adapted a device-independent, 2-D & 3-D graphics package to numerous graphics output devices, including monitors, pen plotters, and printers from many different vendors. For each device we wrote a customized driver implementing kernel-level graphic functions, and thus allowed the same applications to run and produce output on numerous platforms.

In the world of sponsored research, often the development equipment belonged to the sponsor and was delivered on-site at the end of the project. If you didn't develop device-independent capabilities, then your expertise and demonstratable results shipped out the door along with the hardware. More importantly these standards for project portability insured that applications would transfer up to new generation hardware without requiring a re-write.
