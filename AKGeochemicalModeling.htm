The Alaska DGGS Geochemical Modeling System was a joint project of ERDAS and GTRI for the Department of Geology & Geophysical Survey of the state of Alaska. The project combined polygon GIS analysis (MOSS), raster GIS, image processing, geochemical analysis, and cartographic output. My responsibilities involved spec'ing and implementing the vector digitizing modules, coordinating the activities of subcontractor Autometrics, Inc. for the integration of MOSS with the geochemical modeling software, and the implementation and testing of map projections software. We worked on-site at the University of Alaska, Fairbanks during the summers of 1983 and 1985.



Integration of technologies may be an overused term, but for this project it had overriding significance. At the time of this project there may not have been another technical community which could have integrated these technologies as successfully. There were perhaps groups around the country who may have been stronger in vector GIS, there may have been groups stronger in image processing, groups stronger in cartographic output, groups stronger in database applications, stronger in remote sensing, and so on. But I dare say that there was no other group capable of integrating all those technologies into one system and have data genuinely flow in and out of each part in such a workable manner. And recall that this was still a time when many experts thought that only large machines could process such applications, and we broke significant ground in showing that mini- and micro-computers provided the best configuration for delivering operational applications to a wide base of end users.
