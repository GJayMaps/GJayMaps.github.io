Field Data Unit

 

Pacific Bell

San Ramon, CA

San Jose, CA

 

1994-1997

 

Griff Jay

gcon@peak.org

Pacific Bell field engineers mapped poles, lines, and infrastructure for Broadband construction using Field Data Units. The FDUs were Windows-based, Toshiba pen computers pre-loaded with ESRI Arc/Info landbase.

I wrote custom FDU software in VB and C/C++. It overlaid engineering drawings (AutoCad format) onto the neighborhood street/parcel maps. When the engineers entered new CAD objects, the software performed numerous automated checks for CAD validation, geographic placement, and business practice rules.

When the engineers left the field, they returned to the design center with a completed, verified, and validated CAD drawing. The digital CAD file was loaded directly into the Broadband design system. This bypassed previous time-consuming manual digitizing and review steps.
